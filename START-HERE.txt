==================================================================
  Paleoscope - Fossil Globe
  Explore fossil sites from around the world on a 3D globe.
==================================================================

You need an internet connection (the fossil data and photos are
fetched live as you browse).


HOW TO START IT
------------------------------------------------------------------

EASIEST WAY (any computer):
  Just double-click the file:   index.html
  It opens in your web browser. That's it.

IF THE FOSSILS DON'T LOAD with the easy way, use the launcher for
your system instead (it runs a tiny local server, which some
browsers prefer):

  Windows :  double-click   Start-Paleoscope-Windows.bat
  Mac     :  double-click   Start-Paleoscope-Mac.command
  Linux   :  run            ./Start-Paleoscope-Linux.sh

  (On Mac, the first time you may need to right-click the .command
   file and choose "Open" to get past the security prompt.)

A black window/terminal will appear - that is the server.
Leave it open while you use Paleoscope; close it when you're done.


HOW TO USE IT
------------------------------------------------------------------
  * Type a fossil group in "Taxon name" (try: Tyrannosaurus,
    Trilobita, Mammalia, Ammonoidea) and press Search.
  * Narrow it down by geological period, age, or environment.
  * Tick "Only search the area I'm looking at" to search just the
    part of the globe on screen.
  * Spin and zoom the globe with your mouse. The dots are fossil
    sites, coloured by their geological age (see the legend).
  * Click a dot to see what was found there, with photos and
    links to Wikipedia, fossil images and life reconstructions.
  * Tick "Show on ancient Earth" to move every site to where it
    sat on the planet at the time.


PUT IT ON YOUR PHONE (optional)
------------------------------------------------------------------
Paleoscope can be installed on a phone's home screen like an app.

  1. On a computer, start it with the launcher for your system
     (see above) - it will show an address like  http://localhost:8000
  2. Find that computer's network address (its IP), then on your
     phone (same Wi-Fi) open:   http://<that-computer-ip>:8000
  3. Add it to your home screen:
        iPhone (Safari) : Share button -> "Add to Home Screen"
        Android (Chrome): menu -> "Install app"

It then opens full-screen with its own icon. (You still need an
internet connection for the fossil data and photos.)


WHERE THE DATA COMES FROM
------------------------------------------------------------------
  * Fossil records : The Paleobiology Database (paleobiodb.org)
  * Silhouettes    : PhyloPic (phylopic.org), via PBDB
  * Photos & info  : Wikipedia

See README.md for technical details and server deployment notes.
